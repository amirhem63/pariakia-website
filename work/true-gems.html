True Gems —
Building the digital architecture of trust for a high-value purchase category.

A systematic redesign of the trust signals, personalisation tools, and purchase flows that convert a £500–£3,000 jewellery decision from aspiration into confident action.

Role

Senior Product Designer (UX + UI)

Scope

E-commerce Strategy, UX Research, UI Design, Product Personalisation

Platform

Shopify Plus · Web · Mobile

Year

Client

True Gems

Outcome

+38% Conversion Rate, +£62 AOV

01 — Problem

Trust-dependent category. Trust-absent experience.

Fine jewellery is the most trust-dependent category in e-commerce. A customer spending £500–£3,000 on a piece needs absolute confidence in authenticity, quality, and the purchasing experience — confidence that is trivially established in a physical store through the weight of metal in the hand and a sales associate who knows the provenance of every piece. Digitally, True Gems had none of these reassurances in place.

Certification information required three clicks to find. The personalisation tool — allowing customers to customise ring size, metal, and engraving — was technically functional but experientially broken, with a 78% abandonment rate before configuration completion. Users were arriving, selecting a product, attempting to personalise it, and leaving — repeatedly, at scale. The cart was being filled and abandoned not because the product lacked appeal, but because the process of making it theirs felt unreliable.

The checkout compounded the problem. No trust indicators were visible at the point of payment. The returns policy — critical for a high-value gift purchase — was hidden in an FAQ accordion at the bottom of the page. The cumulative effect was a digital experience that had done the hard work of attracting a high-intent customer, then systematically eroded their confidence at every decision point thereafter.

02 — Strategic Thinking

A trust architecture problem, not a visual one.

The visual identity was already strong. The photography was exceptional. What was missing was a systematic approach to building confidence at every micro-decision point in the purchase journey. The design problem was diagnosed as architectural — the absence of a trust layer that would run consistently through every interaction from first impression to post-purchase.

The approach drew on research into high-consideration purchase psychology. In categories where the risk of error feels significant — wrong size, wrong metal, wrong provenance — users require permission to proceed at each stage. That permission comes from confidence signals: evidence that the brand anticipates the concern and has addressed it. The redesign treated trust as a UX layer with its own component library, as fundamental to the design system as typography or colour.

Principle 01

Trust as UX Layer

Every decision point in the purchase journey requires a corresponding confidence signal. A trust component library was established as a first-class element of the design system — present at every critical touchpoint.

Principle 02

Customisation as Discovery

The personalisation tool reimagined from a form to a visual experience — showing the customer what they were creating in real-time. Configuration becomes craft: watching a piece become uniquely yours is the product experience.

Principle 03

Price Transparency as Conversion

Rather than obscuring the cost of customisation options, live pricing would be designed to communicate value-for-money. A customer who understands what they are paying for converts. A customer who fears a hidden charge does not.

03 — UX Design Approach

Mapping every confidence gap, then closing it.

The design process began with a systematic confidence audit — not a standard UX audit, but a deliberate mapping of every moment in the purchase journey where a user might feel doubt. Seventeen distinct confidence gaps were identified, spanning discovery, product detail, personalisation, checkout, and post-purchase. Each was assigned a trust signal type: informational, social, procedural, or visual.

The personalisation tool received the most significant structural redesign. Research sessions with existing customers revealed that the primary source of abandonment was not technical difficulty — it was uncertainty. Users did not trust that what they configured would match what they received. The solution was visual fidelity: a real-time rendered preview that updated instantly with every configuration change. Users could see their piece before they committed to it, eliminating the imaginative leap that had been causing abandonment.

The information architecture was restructured to place trust signals at the point of maximum relevance. Sizing and returns information surfaced inline on product pages, not in help documentation. GIA certification and hallmark details repositioned to the product hero. Craftsmanship context — the artisan story — positioned immediately before the add-to-cart action, providing a final confidence signal at the highest-intent moment.

04 — UI Design System

A visual language that earns confidence at first glance.

The design system was built around one question: what does trustworthy feel like at a visual level? The answer combined precious material references — platinum, gold, white gold — with typographic precision and a spatial generosity that signals unhurried confidence. The typography was set with tighter tracking than conventional web retail to communicate craftsmanship; precise letterforms reflecting precise metalwork.

05 — Key Features & Solutions

Where the architecture became the advantage.

Certification Spotlight Module

GIA certification details and hallmark information redesigned as a visual feature displayed prominently on every product detail page — treated with the same prominence as a product hero image. Certification as credential, not fine print. Trust signal visibility increased from 12% to 94% of product page visits.

Live Customisation Previewer

A real-time visual rendering tool that updates the product preview instantly as customers configure metal type, stone, size, and engraving. The experience of personalisation is transformed from form completion to craft — watching a piece become uniquely yours. Personalisation abandonment reduced from 78% to 22%.

Craftsmanship Video Integration

A 30-second artisan process video embedded per product — showing the hands that make each piece. Positioned immediately before the add-to-cart action, it provides a final confidence signal at the moment of maximum intent. Products with video integration converted at 1.9× the rate of those without.

Transparent Pricing Accordion

A clear cost breakdown for all customisation options — metal premium, stone upgrade, engraving — displayed inline before configuration is selected. Live total price updates with each selection. Pricing transparency designed as a value communication tool: understanding what you are paying for is reassuring, not deterring.

Inline Returns & Sizing Guide

Sizing information and the returns policy integrated directly into the product page — accessible via a compact inline expansion, without navigating away from the PDP. Critical reassurance at the point where uncertainty is highest, without disrupting the purchase flow.

Post-Purchase Concierge CTA

A post-confirmation touchpoint connecting buyers to care guides, aftercare services, and a direct contact for gifting questions. Extending the brand relationship beyond the transaction — and generating a 44% increase in return customer rate through investment in post-purchase experience.

06 — Interaction & Motion

Motion calibrated to precision and weight.

The motion system for a fine jewellery brand demands a specific character: unhurried, precise, and weighty. Transitions are slow where speed would feel cheap — product image transitions use a 600ms crossfade rather than a snap. The customisation previewer renders with a brief, deliberate pause between selections — designed to suggest that something real is happening, that the piece is being considered before it is shown.

The certification spotlight module uses a subtle reveal animation on scroll — the hallmark details appearing to materialise as the user reaches them — referencing the physical experience of a piece being handed across a counter for inspection. Interaction patterns throughout were calibrated to create a sense of care and deliberation: this is a brand that is not in a hurry, because the products it makes are worth taking time over.

The live pricing display — updated with each customisation selection — uses a counter animation rather than a flash replacement, communicating that the price is being recalculated rather than simply overwritten. A small design choice with a significant psychological effect: it makes the pricing feel considered rather than arbitrary.

07 — Impact & Outcome

Trust, measured.

+38% Overall conversion rate uplift (14-week post-launch)
+£62 Average order value increase
78%→22% Personalisation tool abandonment rate
+44% Return customer rate

The personalisation tool transformation was the most commercially significant outcome. A feature that had been a documented pain point — attracting users and then losing them — became the highest-performing conversion mechanism on the site. The AOV increase of £62 was attributable primarily to increased customisation completion: customers who completed personalisation spent significantly more than those who purchased off-the-shelf.

08 — Reflection

What this project taught.

What worked

The diagnostic reframe — from visual problem to trust architecture problem — was the most valuable contribution of the early project phase. It changed the scope and the methodology. Without that reframe, the work would have produced a more attractive version of an experience that still failed to convert at the critical decision points. The confidence audit process became a replicable method subsequently applied to other high-consideration purchase categories.

What would evolve

The craftsmanship video integration was implemented at the product level — one video per product. A future iteration would introduce artisan profiles: the specific maker behind a piece, with their story and craft context. Research strongly suggests that buyer-maker connection is a significant emotional driver in fine jewellery. The technology exists; the content production investment would need to be made alongside it.

Trade-offs made

The decision to place the returns policy prominently on the product page — rather than in a help section — was contested by the commercial team, who were concerned it would draw attention to the possibility of return. The data post-launch showed the opposite effect: surfaces the policy increased purchase confidence rather than surfacing doubt. A counter-intuitive outcome that demonstrates the importance of testing assumptions with real users rather than commercial intuition.

Design leadership dimension

This project required sustained advocacy for research-based decisions against commercially intuitive alternatives. The insight that transparency — about pricing, about processes, about policies — builds rather than undermines conversion confidence is well evidenced in the literature but feels risky to act on without data. Building the case through usability research before any visual work began was what made the final recommendations defensible.

"The personalisation tool used to be our biggest frustration — users loved the idea but couldn't complete it. Now it's our biggest conversion driver. The difference is clarity."
— E-commerce Director, True Gems
