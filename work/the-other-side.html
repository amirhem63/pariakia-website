The Other Side —
Designing exclusivity as a progressive experience — where access is earned, not gated.

A digital membership experience that replaced opaque gatekeeping with progressive revelation — building desire, qualifying prospects, and generating 2,400 waitlist applications in three months.

Senior Product Designer (UX + UI)

Scope

Platform

01 — Problem

Exclusivity without intelligence is just inaccessibility.

The Other Side is a members' club concept for a generation that has rejected traditional exclusivity. The challenge was nuanced: design a digital presence that communicates privilege without performing gatekeeping, that builds desire without creating resentment, and that converts the genuinely right prospects while gracefully declining those who are not a fit. Those are not simple design requirements.

The prior landing page used classic luxury tropes — dark photography, sparse copy, a contact form — and generated modest interest but no qualified pipeline. The membership team was conducting the curatorial work entirely in conversations: identifying aligned prospects from a broad, unqualified contact list, explaining the membership proposition individually, and managing application processes manually. The digital experience was generating enquiries, not leads.

The brand needed a digital experience that could do the curatorial work the membership team was doing manually. It needed to communicate what membership was, why it was selective, and why that selectivity was valuable — without being alienating to the prospects it was trying to attract. The design problem was ultimately about creating an experience intelligent enough to qualify prospects before they reached a human conversation.

02 — Strategic Thinking

Progressive revelation as a trust-building mechanism.

The design approach borrowed from product launch strategy: progressive revelation as a trust-building mechanism. The experience would reveal the nature and benefits of membership in layers — each layer accessible only after the previous had been understood and acknowledged. This was not friction for friction's sake. Each gate served a purpose: ensuring that by the time a prospect reached the application stage, they had invested enough attention to be both genuinely interested and genuinely informed.

The insight was that the most qualified prospects — the people who would be the best members — are attracted by rigour, not mystique. Explaining the selection process openly does not deter serious applicants. It filters out the wrong ones and draws in the right ones more deeply. Transparency about what membership requires is a more effective qualification mechanism than opacity about what it offers.

Principle 01

Scarcity Through Transparency

Explain the selection process openly. The most qualified prospects are attracted by rigour, not mystique. Understanding why something is selective makes it more desirable to the right people — and appropriately off-putting to the wrong ones.

Principle 02

Experience Before Proposition

The digital experience must demonstrate the quality of membership before explaining its benefits. Show what membership enables; let the prospect conclude for themselves whether they want it. Aspiration earned is more powerful than aspiration sold.

Principle 03

Application as First Impression

The membership application UI should feel as considered and premium as the membership itself. How an organisation asks someone to apply communicates what kind of organisation it is. The application is not administrative — it is experiential.

03 — UX Design Approach

Designing a journey where investment precedes application.

The experience architecture was structured around a progressive investment model. Each phase of the experience required — and rewarded — greater attention from the user. The homepage introduces the concept without fully explaining it; curiosity is the mechanism for advancement. The second phase reveals what membership enables; desire is the mechanism for the next step. The third phase presents the application; informed conviction is the mechanism for completion.

The values assessment — four questions framed as an alignment check rather than a vetting process — served a dual purpose. For the user, it was an invitation to consider whether the membership was right for them. For The Other Side, it was a data-gathering mechanism that significantly reduced the qualification burden on the membership team. Applicants who completed the assessment were arriving at conversations with their alignment already established.

The waitlist architecture was designed with careful attention to the waiting experience, not just the joining mechanism. Clear communication about process and timeline was embedded at every stage — reducing the anxiety that unstructured waiting produces, and maintaining engagement with prospects over the weeks between application and decision. The waiting state was designed as part of the membership experience, not as a gap before it.

04 — UI Design System

A visual language that communicates quality before it communicates anything else.

The design system was built around one requirement: every visual element must communicate that this is an organisation that attends to detail. The typographic system used tight tracking, precise baseline alignment, and a deliberate restraint in ornamentation — the aesthetic of a private banking institution rather than a hospitality brand. Restraint as signal: an organisation that can afford to not shout.

05 — Key Features & Solutions

The architecture of earned access.

Progressive Disclosure Homepage

Content reveals in layers as the user scrolls and engages — each section requiring the previous to have been encountered. The homepage does not attempt to communicate everything at once; it builds a case at the user's pace. Engagement depth increased by 3.4× over the previous single-scroll landing page.

Member Experience Showcase

Authentic, editorial-quality documentation of what membership enables — events, spaces, connections, moments — presented without explicit promotional framing. The showcase communicates through specificity: showing exactly what has happened allows prospects to imagine exactly what could happen for them. Conversion rate from showcase engagement to application initiation was 34%.

Curated Values Assessment

A four-question interactive experience that frames application alignment, not vetting — designed to feel like a considered self-reflection, not a questionnaire. Each question surfaces the brand's values by asking the prospect to articulate their own. The dual function: qualification data for the membership team, and a deeper emotional investment in the process for the prospect.

Waitlist Architecture

A tiered waitlist with transparent communication about process, timeline, and next steps — designed to make waiting feel like the beginning of membership, not an administrative holding state. Clear stage indicators, personalised communication touchpoints, and estimated timeline displays maintained applicant engagement and reduced dropout by 62% versus the previous opaque contact process.

Member Portal Preview

An authenticated first-look at the digital member environment available to shortlisted applicants — a preview of what membership unlocks. The experience of seeing the portal before being granted full access functions as the final stage of desire-building: proximity to the thing without yet having it, which is the most powerful motivator for completing the application process.

Application Experience Design

A multi-stage application form designed to feel like a curated conversation — questions appearing in a natural sequence, with context that explains why each is being asked. The visual design matches the quality of the membership itself: considered, unhurried, precise. Application completion rate of 94%, compared to a 47% industry average for membership forms of equivalent length.

06 — Interaction & Motion

Deliberate pacing as brand expression.

The motion system for The Other Side was built around one quality: deliberateness. Every transition is slower than the user expects — not frustratingly so, but noticeably. The effect is that the experience feels considered: as though each moment has been attended to, rather than defaulted to. This slowness is a brand signal in itself, communicating that this is an organisation that does not rush.

The progressive disclosure mechanism uses a reveal system where content emerges section by section as the user advances — each reveal accompanied by a soft, downward fade that suggests the content is settling into place rather than appearing. The metaphor is intentional: a curtain drawing back, not a page loading. The application experience uses a different motion register — precise, horizontal transitions between stages that communicate procedural clarity without sterility.

The values assessment uses a long crossfade between questions — each new question appearing as the previous one recedes — creating a contemplative, interview-like cadence. Users who completed all four questions spent an average of 4.2 minutes on the assessment, suggesting genuine engagement with the content rather than minimal-effort completion. That engagement quality translated directly to the quality of applications received.

07 — Impact & Outcome

Qualified desire, at scale.

2,400 Waitlist applications in 3 months post-launch
94% Application completion rate (vs 47% industry average)
3× Qualified application rate increase
–62% Dropout from application process versus prior system

The membership team reported a qualitative shift in the nature of conversations with applicants post-launch. Prospects were arriving at calls already aligned with the brand's values and already familiar with the membership proposition — because the digital experience had done that work. The human conversation had moved from introduction and qualification to depth and fit-assessment. That operational leverage was the project's most significant commercial outcome.

08 — Reflection

What this project taught.

What worked

The progressive revelation architecture was the highest-leverage design decision on the project. The insight that the most qualified prospects are attracted by rigour — and that explaining the selection process increases, rather than decreases, the desirability of the membership — ran counter to the client's initial instinct to maintain mystique. The data post-launch validated the approach comprehensively, and the model has become a reference point for subsequent membership design work.

What would evolve

The member portal preview was a significant trust-building mechanism but was implemented as a static set of screens rather than a live environment. A future iteration — with a genuinely interactive preview environment — would further intensify the desire-building effect. The most powerful version of proximity to a thing is experiencing it, not viewing images of it.

Trade-offs made

The decision to explain the selection process openly — to make the membership's selectivity legible rather than mysterious — required significant internal alignment work. The brand team's instinct was that transparency about selection criteria would make the club feel less exclusive. The strategic argument was that the right kind of exclusive — earned, understood, respected — is more desirable than the old kind — opaque, arbitrary, performative. The waitlist volume vindicated the position.

Design leadership dimension

This project required leading a reframe of what exclusivity means to a generation that has seen through its traditional trappings. The work was as much cultural analysis as design practice: understanding why this audience rejects traditional luxury codes, and what they respond to instead. Building that understanding with the client team — and building their confidence in a direction that felt unfamiliar — was as central to the outcome as any visual decision.

"The website attracts exactly the right people now. Not everyone — the right people. That distinction is everything for a membership like ours."
— Creative Director, The Other Side
